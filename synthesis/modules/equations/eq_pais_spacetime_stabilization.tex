\begin{equation}
  \nabla^2 \phi - \frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial t^2} + V'(\phi) = -\frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu} g^{\mu\nu} = -\rho
  \eqtag{P}{STAB}{SCALAR}
  \label{eq:pais:spacetime-stabilization}
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{Stabilization Mechanism:} Scalar fields $\phi$ stabilize nonlinear spacetime perturbations generated by the Superforce by providing a dynamic energy reservoir. The potential gradient $V'(\phi)$ acts as a restoring force preventing runaway curvature fluctuations, while the source term $\rho$ couples the scalar to matter/energy density.

\noindent\textbf{Integration with GEM:} The scalar field equation couples to the gravitoelectromagnetic (GEM) stress-energy tensor via:
\begin{equation*}
  \rho_{\mathrm{eff}} = \rho_{\mathrm{matter}} + \rho_{\mathrm{GEM}} + \rho_{\phi}
\end{equation*}
where $\rho_{\mathrm{GEM}} = -g^2/(8\pi G)$ is the negative gravitational energy density and $\rho_{\phi} = (\nabla\phi)^2/2 + V(\phi)$ is the scalar field energy.

\noindent\textbf{Curvature Modulation:} The missing mechanism in Pais's original formulation is provided by scalar field mediation, enabling sustained coherence required for Superforce validation and practical applications. The equation admits stable solitonic solutions representing localized regions of enhanced Superforce activity.
