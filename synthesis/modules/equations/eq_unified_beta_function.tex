\begin{equation}
  \beta(\lambda) = \mu\frac{d\lambda}{d\mu} = \frac{1}{16\pi^2}\left[3\lambda^2 - \sum_i g_i^2\lambda + \sum_j y_j^4\right] + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^3)
  \eqtag{U}{QM}{T}
  \label{eq:unified:beta-function}
\end{equation}
% Notes: Renormalization group beta function describing the energy-scale dependence of
% coupling constants in the unified framework. Here $\lambda$ represents the quartic
% scalar self-coupling, $\mu$ is the renormalization scale, $g_i$ are gauge couplings
% (corresponding to electromagnetic, weak, and strong interactions in the Pais framework
% or emergent gauge symmetries in Genesis nodespace), and $y_j$ are Yukawa couplings
% to matter fields. The beta function determines whether couplings grow (UV-incomplete)
% or shrink (asymptotically free) at high energies. A zero of $\beta(\lambda)$ indicates
% a fixed point, potentially corresponding to scale-invariant physics. Cross-framework
% validation requires consistent beta functions across Aether, Genesis, and Pais descriptions.
