\begin{equation}
  F_{\mathrm{S}} = \frac{c^4}{G} \approx 1.21 \times 10^{44} \text{ N}

\noindent\textbf{Physical Interpretation:} The Superforce represents the Planck Force that governs spacetime-matter interactions at the most fundamental level. This force emerges naturally from dimensional analysis of Einstein's field equations, where the coupling constant $(8\pi G/c^4)$ has dimensions of inverse force. The magnitude $1.21 \times 10^{44}$ N represents the maximum force achievable in nature when gravitational and quantum effects merge at the Planck scale.

\noindent\textbf{Key Insight:} This formulation lacks $\hbar$, suggesting the Superforce relates to Macroscopic Quantum Phenomena exhibiting classical behavior despite quantum origins.
