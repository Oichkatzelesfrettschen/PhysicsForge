1. FIRST EXPERIMENTAL DEMONSTRATION - Monroe Group (2017)
   --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
   Citation: J. Zhang, P. W. Hess, A. Kyprianidis, P. Becker, A. Lee, J. Smith,
             G. Pagano, I. D. Potirniche, A. C. Potter, A. Vishwanath, N. Y. Yao,
             and C. Monroe
             "Observation of a discrete time crystal"
             Nature 543, 217-220 (2017)

   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/nature21413

   Experimental Platform:
   - System: Chain of 10 trapped 171Yb+ ions
   - Confinement: Paul trap with radio-frequency electromagnetic fields
   - Temperature: Laser-cooled ions

   Key Observables Measured:
   - Subharmonic oscillation of the drive frequency
   - Period-doubling response (oscillation at 2T where T = drive period)
   - Robust synchronization across ion chain

   Coherence/Lifetime Data:
   - Oscillations persisted for >100 cycles
   - Relatively long-lived spin coherence in nitrogen-vacancy centers
   - Limited by decoherence and heating effects

   Theoretical Framework:
   - Many-body localization (MBL) in disordered system
   - Ising spin chain with transverse and longitudinal fields
   - Floquet theory for periodically driven systems

   Anomalous Behavior:
   - Rigid subharmonic response insensitive to perturbations
   - Synchronization over macroscopic distances
   - Eigenstate order throughout spectrum


2. PRETHERMAL DISCRETE TIME CRYSTAL - Kyprianidis et al. (2021)
   --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
   Citation: A. Kyprianidis et al.
             "Observation of a prethermal discrete time crystal"
             Science 372, 6547, pp. 1192-1196 (2021)
             Published: June 11, 2021

   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1126/science.abg8102
   arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.01695

   Experimental Platform:
   - System: 25 trapped ion qubits (quantum simulator)
   - Stabilization: High-frequency driving (disorder-free)

   Key Observables:
   - Prethermal discrete time crystal signatures
   - Drive-frequency control of lifetime
   - Energy density dependence of time-crystalline order

   Coherence/Lifetime:
   - Lifetime controlled by drive frequency
   - Heating circumvented by prethermalization, not MBL

   Theoretical Framework:
   - Prethermalization mechanism (alternative to MBL)
   - High-frequency Floquet driving
   - No disorder required (clean system)

   Key Distinction:
   - Unlike MBL time crystals, prethermal DTCs don't require disorder
   - Finite lifetime set by drive frequency
   - Initial state energy density matters

1. RUBIDIUM BEC IN OPTICAL CAVITY - First Continuous Time Crystal (2022)
   --------------------------------------------------------------------------------
   Citation: H. Kessler et al.
             "Observation of a continuous time crystal"
             Science 377, 6606, pp. 670-673 (2022)
             Published: June 2022

   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1126/science.abo3382
   arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.06980 (Feb 14, 2022 - Aug 23, 2022)

   Experimental Platform:
   - System: Rubidium Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC)
   - Environment: High-finesse optical cavity
   - Drive: Continuous pumping (time-independent)
   - Temperature: Microkelvin regime

   Key Observables Measured:
   - Spontaneous limit cycle oscillations
   - Emergent oscillations in intracavity photon number
   - Random phase (0 to 2pi) for different realizations
   - Atomic density cycling through recurring patterns

   Coherence/Lifetime:
   - Continuous oscillations sustained during pumping
   - Coherent state preserved for several minutes
   - Phase randomness confirms spontaneous symmetry breaking

   Theoretical Framework:
   - Dissipative atom-cavity system
   - Continuous time translation symmetry breaking (not discrete)
   - Limit cycle phase with emergent frequency
   - Not subharmonic response - intrinsic oscillation frequency

   Key Innovation:
   - First experimental continuous time crystal
   - Time-independent drive (breaks continuous, not discrete symmetry)
   - Random phase demonstrates true spontaneous breaking

   Anomalous Behavior:
   - Robust limit cycles against temporal perturbations
   - Self-sustained oscillations without periodic driving
   - Phase completely randomized between realizations

   Research Team:
   - Hans Kessler and Andreas Hemmerich (supervisors)
   - Institute of Laser Physics, University of Hamburg

   - Hours-long coherence in electron-nuclear systems
   - Spin maser: Self-sustained oscillations


ENERGY STABILITY MEASUREMENTS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Key Finding: Time crystals possess "motion without energy"
- Apparent motion does not represent conventional kinetic energy
- Already in quantum ground state or dissipative steady state
- Cannot lose energy to environment (ground state)
- Energy input balances dissipation (driven systems)

Stability Mechanisms:
1. Many-body localization (prevents thermalization)
2. Prethermalization (high-frequency driving)
3. Dissipative balance (driven-dissipative systems)
4. Topological protection
5. Superfluidity (superfluid time crystals)

LIMITATIONS & CHALLENGES:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Thermalization Suppression:
   - MBL requires finely tuned conditions
   - Experimentally demanding
   - Finite-size effects

2. Decoherence:
   - Magnetic noise limits coherence
   - Environmental coupling
   - Finite qubit lifetimes

3. Atom Loss:
   - Important future task: overcome atom loss
   - Limits practical lifetime

4. Finite Coherence Times:
   - Google experiment: hundreds of cycles (not indefinite)
   - Limited by hardware imperfections
