--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
   Citation: X. Mi et al. (Google AI Quantum and collaborators)
             "Time-crystalline eigenstate order on a quantum processor"
             Nature 601, 531-536 (2022)

   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-021-04257-w
   arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.13571

   Experimental Platform:
   - System: Google Sycamore superconducting quantum processor
   - Qubits: Array of superconducting qubits
   - Gate operations: Tunable CPHASE gates

   Key Observables Measured:
   - Eigenstate-ordered discrete time crystal (DTC) phase
   - Quantum correlations throughout many-body spectrum
   - Long-range order in late-time dynamics from all initial states

   Coherence/Lifetime:
   - Time crystal oscillations observed for ~100-200 cycles
   - Limited by finite coherence time of superconducting qubits
   - Typical qubit coherence: T1 ~ 15-30 microseconds, T2 ~ 10-20 microseconds

   Theoretical Framework:
   - Eigenstate order in periodically driven MBL systems
   - Out-of-equilibrium dynamical phases
   - Time-reversal protocol to discriminate decoherence from thermalization
   - Quantum typicality to sample eigenspectrum

   Key Innovation:
   - Scalable approach for engineering DTC phases
   - Continuous family of tunable interactions
   - Demonstrates eigenstate order (not just a few eigenstates)

   Anomalous Behavior:
   - Entire many-body spectrum exhibits coherent oscillations
   - Characteristic signatures persist in late-time dynamics
   - Robust against typical quantum processor noise
