1.1 DESI (DARK ENERGY SPECTROSCOPIC INSTRUMENT) 2024 RESULTS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Citation: "Quantifying Scalar Field Dynamics with DESI 2024 Y1 BAO
measurements" (2024)
arXiv: 2404.14341
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.14341

Key Findings:
- DESI BAO measurements indicate preference for evolving dark energy over
  cosmological constant
- Statistical significance: 2.6-3.9 sigma when combined with other probes
- Best fit model: 2-4% kinetic scalar field energy for canonical scalar field
- Potentials tested: Quadratic and linear potentials

Experimental Constraints:
- Three simple scalar field models directly fit to DESI BAO + CMB + SNe data
- Only DES-Y5 supernova combination showed preference for quintessence over
  Lambda-CDM at 95% CL
- Exponential quintessence faces challenges in simultaneously fitting SNe,
  DESI, and CMB data

Future Prospects:
- First year cosmology data release planned for 2026
- Will provide tighter constraints on scalar field dynamics

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Citation: "DESI 2024: Constraints on Physics-Focused Aspects of Dark Energy
using DESI DR1 BAO Data" (2024)
arXiv: 2405.13588
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.13588

Key Findings:
- Explored three physics-focused dark energy behaviors:
  * Thawing class (matching quintessence potentials)
  * Emergent class (recently arising dark energy)
  * Mirage class
- Preference for thawing dark energy scenarios with parameterized equations
  of state

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Citation: "DESI Constraints on Exponential Quintessence" (2024)
arXiv: 2405.18747
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.18747

Coupling Strengths:
- No preference for exponential quintessence over Lambda-CDM within specific
  field theory model
- Challenge: Slowly-evolving dark energy equation of state lacks freedom to
  fit combined datasets

URL: https://journals.aps.org/prd/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevD.101.044020

Model: Matter-coupled scalar field
Interaction Potential: Symmetry-breaking form with two potential wells

Key Parameters:
- Cosmological constant constraint satisfied
- Fifth force strength LARGE compared to gravity
- Short interaction range due to density dependence

Force Strength and Range (ambient density):
- Coupling coefficient ~ 10^31 (inversely proportional to ambient density)
- Interaction range ~ 5 micrometers (inversely proportional to sqrt(density))
- For current Universe matter density

Challenge:
- Despite strong coupling, short range makes local gravity tests pass
- Extremely difficult experimental detection
