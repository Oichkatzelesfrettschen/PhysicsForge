6.1 Introduction to Cayley-Dickson Algebras

The Cayley-Dickson construction provides a recursive framework to generate higher-dimensional algebras from simpler ones. Beginning with the real numbers ( \mathbb{R} ), this construction successively produces complex numbers ( \mathbb{C} ), quaternions ( \mathbb{H} ), octonions ( \mathbb{O} ), and beyond, introducing new dimensions while progressively losing algebraic properties such as commutativity and associativity.

The Cayley-Dickson construction doubles the dimension of an algebra at each step:
\[
\mathbb{R} \xrightarrow{2D} \mathbb{C} \xrightarrow{4D} \mathbb{H} \xrightarrow{8D} \mathbb{O} \xrightarrow{16D} \mathbb{S} \xrightarrow{32D} \mathbb{P} \xrightarrow{\dots} 2^n\text{D}.
\]

Each step in the Cayley-Dickson process results in the loss of certain algebraic properties:
	1.	Associativity is retained only up to quaternions.
	2.	Alternativity is preserved in octonions but breaks for sedenions.
	3.	Normed division is lost entirely beyond octonions, replaced by semi-normed systems.
